FACTOIDS

Human Bones

Babies are born with around 300 bones, but many fuse together as they grow up. Adults have 206 bones.

Raining Diamonds

The pressure in the atmospheres of Neptune, Uranus, and Saturn can cause carbon atoms to crystallize into diamonds.

Bananas

Bananas are slightly radioactive because they contain potassium, which decays.

Lizards

Lizards use their tongues to smell by catching scent particles in the air and placing them on the roof of their mouths.

Grasshoppers

Grasshoppers have ears in their bellies.

Killer whales

Killer whales are actually dolphins because they have a fatty deposit called a melon that helps them with echolocation.

Neutron Stars

A teaspoon of neutron star would weigh 6 billion tons.

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower grows in the summer because substances expand when heated.

Cloud Weight

A cloud weighs around a million tonnes. A cloud typically has a volume of around 1km3 and a density of around 1.003kg per m3 – that's a density that’s around 0.4 per cent lower than the air surrounding it.
